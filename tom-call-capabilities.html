Perch · Product framing

Call Cockpit → Tom

The standalone call cockpit, decomposed into two capability sets for Tom to absorb inside Perch. Call Prep is the research & understanding Tom does before and during a call; Call Handling is how Tom tracks, notes, and classifies it. Each line is a capability — what Tom does, stated plainly.

● Call Prep

Research & understand the target 12

Before & during the call — the left rail + profile.
  • Rank the call list by a composite Perch Score — Procore activity, typical job size, locality, ICP fit, and contactability.
  • Judge first-principles fit — does this company actually bid & scope commercial construction (would it ever get value from, or buy, Perch)? Tag Strong / Moderate / not-a-fit with a reason.
  • Enrich from the open web — verify the real website, LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, founding year, employee count, and specialties, with a confidence rating.
  • Write a grounded TL;DR — a source-verified, 2–3 sentence summary of what they build, their scale, and notable work. Never invented.
  • Size the company from real signals (employees, revenue, scale cues), not misleading proxies like software-usage counts.
  • Surface the Procore signal — total & active projects, average job value, estimated volume, trades, and market sectors.
  • Assemble a single call card — profile, click-to-call phone, enrichment, stats, and fit reason in one view.
  • Offer one-click research launchers — website, socials, Google, Maps, and the Procore profile.
  • Auto-route the right script by contractor type (GC vs. sub) and size (small / medium / large), with instant override.
  • Generate the discovery script — opener variants, the three Mom-Test questions + follow-ups, size-tailored asks & "listen-for" cues, the matched GTM wedge, a one-question fallback, and an objection-handling library.
  • Flag data-quality catches found while enriching — rebrands, misleading trade tags, wrong-entity matches, out-of-area HQs.
  • Search & filter the queue by name, trade, ICP, size, fit, and call status to work the right segment.
● Call Handling

Track, note & classify 8

During & after the call — the right rail.
  • Classify the outcome into a standard disposition: productive, non-productive, gatekeeper, left voicemail, wrong person, advised-to-email, no answer, callback set, not a fit, or do-not-call.
  • Capture structured discovery notes — contact & title, persona, current tools, objection raised, pain discovered, the question that landed, the verbatim "golden quote," next step, the GTM wedge that resonated, an interest rating, and free-form notes.
  • Keep a per-company call history — a timestamped, reverse-chronological record of every touch.
  • Show live pipeline counters — companies dialed, total calls, productive calls, and callbacks set.
  • Persist automatically — every outcome, note, and script override saved per rep, no login.
  • Export the call log to CSV (all calls + structured fields) for the CRM or analysis.
  • Enforce a shared vocabulary — consistent personas, tools, wedges, and outcomes so notes stay clean and queryable across the team.
  • Turn every logged call into structured, queryable discovery data (persona × trade × objection × pain × quote × wedge) that sharpens scripts and messaging over time.

Note for the build

Call Prep is the heavy lift. In the cockpit these run as offline pipelines — scoring + parallel web-research agents, precomputed into the file. Inside Perch, Tom performs them live, on demand. That's the real agentic work.

Call Handling is mostly conventional. Live capture, persistence, counters, export — standard product work; the uplift is doing it in Perch on a shared backend instead of per-browser storage.

Source of truth: the working app at perch-call-cockpit.pages.dev (75 fit-verified contractors). Green-lit capabilities become the Tom skill spec.
