Building Backwards: $1M by end of Q1, then the ramp to $15M

You asked: is $1M too conservative, and is $1M→$15M in three months insane? Here's the math, laid out so we can pick a customer-makeup we can actually knock down domino by domino. Pricing uses our working ACV model — confirm before external use.

01 The ACV building blocks

Every scenario is built from these six tiers (annual contract value). Q1 = SMB + warm network, so the realistic Q1 blocks are GC Core, Sub Essentials, Sub Growth, with the occasional warm GC Pro.

02 Why $1M is the right Q1 number — and why $15M next isn't crazy

$1M in Q1 is deliberately conservative — because Q1 is about proving the motion, not scaling it.

  • We're still hardening the product and standing up the sales engine. Q1 runs on warm network + SMB self-serve + Built referrals.
  • SMB ACVs are small ($30–84K), so $1M = roughly 15–26 logos. That's winnable from a warm base + first cold wins — without a big team yet.
  • The real Q1 win isn't the dollar — it's the first cold logo outside Chris + a repeatable motion we can pour fuel on.

$1M → $15M is ACV mechanics + headcount, NOT 15× the customers.

  • We move up-market. Q2 opens GCs to $500M rev and subs to $250M — ACVs jump 3–10× ($120K–$400K). One enterprise GC ≈ 10 SMB logos.
  • The team is built by Q2. Q1 proves the playbook; Q2 we have AE/SE pods + CSMs running it (see Sales Engine).
  • The flywheel compounds. Every GC we land drags its subs onto the platform — pull-through we don't pay SDRs for.
  • Illustration: +$14M net-new in Q2 ≈ 40 GC Pro ($4.8M) + 40 Sub Growth ($3.4M) + 8 GC Enterprise ($3.2M) + 8 Sub Enterprise ($1.2M) + ~50 GC Core ($2.1M) ≈ $14.7M from ~146 logos — concentrated up-market, not a linear SMB grind.

This is a living model — re-forecast revenue AND hiring in real time.

  • Every number here is a hypothesis until reps are in the field. The moment we have real demand and real win rates, we recalibrate weekly: the revenue forecast, the pricing dial, and the hiring engine.
  • The hiring engine has its own dials: interviews/week → resumes processed/week → reps ramped/week. If demand outruns capacity (the Wiz signal), we accelerate hiring; if it lags, we slow the burn. Build it flexible from day one.
  • The calculator below and the ramp in the Sales Engine are knobs, not commitments — we turn them as the ground truth comes in.
