The next conversation

Single objective

Turn access into a named estimating-workflow discovery.

Send the promised project context, then ask for 20 minutes with the person who owns commercial estimating. The second call should quantify a problem and map authority—not repeat firmographic qualification.

Find the owner“Who owns commercial estimating and decides how your team runs takeoff and bid review?”
Map the workflow“When a set lands, walk me from invite to submitted number. Who touches it and where does it wait?”
Quantify throughput“How many invitations do you see in a month, and how many can the team seriously price?”
Find the cost“When a bid goes sideways, is the loss estimator hours, missed scope, vendor quotes, addenda, or follow-up?”

E

Reusable scoring rules

Evidence hierarchy
Transcript or recording evidence beats notes. Prospect-stated facts beat seller claims. Named people, dates, amounts, and documented processes beat adjectives. External research can create a hypothesis but cannot qualify an opportunity by itself.
Common false positives
Friendliness is not a Champion. A generic inbox is not a Decision Process. Project contract value is not a Perch value Metric. “We are bonded” is not a complete Paper Process. A seller explaining a pain does not mean the prospect has identified it.
Stage interpretation
0–19: account only; pursue discovery. 20–39: early opportunity hypothesis. 40–59: discovery underway with material gaps. 60–79: qualified opportunity with a credible path. 80–100: validated, multi-threaded, execution-ready. Scores should never advance stage without a dated next step.
Call scoring interpretation
Score every call separately for account fit, conversation yield, and next-step strength. Use MEDDPICC only as the cumulative opportunity evidence ledger. A call can be excellent even when the cumulative MEDDPICC score remains low.
